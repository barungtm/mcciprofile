Geographical Structure

The district is divided into two parts based on the geographical structure.

Central Hilly Region(मध्य पहाड)

This region extends from the high land of Northern part of Mahabharat Parvat Range to Besiphand. Three fourth of the total land is covered by this region. Since the high lands of this region contains cold climate, it is favourable for unseasonal vegetable farming, potato farming, winter fruits, orange etc farming. While the low lands are favourable for potato and other crops farming.

Inner Terai(भित्री तराइ)

Covering one fourth of the total land of district, this region extends from eastern to western part in between the Chure Parvat and Mahabharat Parvat Range. The warm climate of this region is favourable planting crops and some seasonal vegetables.

Major Hill Range(मुख्य पर्वत श्रीङ्खला)
  • Southern part of Chandragiri hill from Fakhel to Tistung towards the north
  • Mahabharat Hill Range from Kogate to Fakhel towards the north center
  • Chure Hill Range from Raigaun to Manahari towards the South
